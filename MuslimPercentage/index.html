The decreasing Muslim population share in India

Approximately 17 out of 100 children in the age group of 0-4 years are Muslims. However there are only 10 Muslims(approx) out of 100 people who are over 80 years of age. Clearly, the Muslim (%) share in the Indian population is decreasing with Age .Or in simple words, Muslims in India seem to be dying earlier. Data shows that this trend is not only for India as a whole, but also across individual states without any exceptions.

One possible explanation can be found in the Monthly per capita expenditure data (MPCE) collected by the NSSO. In 2011, 58 out of every 100 Urban Muslims lived on less then Rs.1075 per month. The numbers were better among the other religions.For example among Urban Hindus 37 out of every 100 lived on less then Rs.1075 per month. However data from Rural areas did not show any significant difference between Muslims and Hindus.
